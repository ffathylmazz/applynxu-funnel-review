01

The short version

I registered, verified, and walked the entire application to the $25 checkout — on desktop and mobile — watching the network and inbox the whole way. The flow is well-built and genuinely low-friction at the top. The losses cluster in two places: a silent dead-end at sign-up, and a heavy identity-verification wall placed right before payment. And the pattern is the wrong shape: failure severity increases as the applicant invests more — cosmetic at register, submission-blocking by identity — the opposite of how a healthy funnel behaves.

What's strong

  • One-screen sign-up · honest "<10 min" promise
  • Google option · passwordless · saved-and-resumable
  • Country-localized doc lists · WhatsApp verify · sponsor invoicing

What's costing starts

  • A completed sign-up can fail with a generic "An error occurred" (unsupported phone country)
  • Document + selfie KYC is front-loaded before any payment

The highest-leverage move

  • Fix the confirmed sign-up bugs now (cheap)
  • Remove file-format friction at identity (medium)
  • Test resequencing KYC to after payment — biggest lift, validate vs fraud first

A note on how I read these. I split every observation into Confirmed — a bug or inconsistency safe to fix — and Validate — a design choice that's probably intentional and needs the team's data before touching. Calling everything "broken" would be the wrong read; some of what looks like friction is deliberate monetization or compliance. And every recommendation ties back to App start → App complete; the two items that aren't direct conversion levers — fixing the tracking (measurement integrity) and the token-in-URL (security) — are labelled as such, so the tie-back stays honest.

02

The funnel, as it actually runs

"App start" is account creation; "App complete" is the $25 non-refundable application fee. Between them sit an email gate and four steps — each a place to lose people.

S0Register Name, email, phone, city, program — or "Continue with Google"App start
S0·5Email verification 6-digit code (Google path skips this)gate
1Previous education Level → degree, year, institution, countrylight
2Personal information 14+ fields: DoB, address, nationality, gender…heavy · form fatigue
3Identity documents Government ID + selfie, image-only, <10MBheaviest · KYC wall
4Application fee checkout $25 · card / PayPal / bank transfer / sponsorApp complete

Highest intent meets highest friction at Step 3 — and the drop there is the most expensive, because the applicant is one click from converting.

Who we're optimizing for — "Ambitious Amaka"

Proto-persona · assumption-based, to validate

~30, Lagos, working full-time. Applies for the online MBA on her phone, after work, on mobile data. Non-technical, cost-sensitive ($25 fee matters). Her ID is a PDF scan, her camera shoots HEIC, her Nigerian card is restricted for USD, and her trust is fragile ("is this US school real?"). Reachable on WhatsApp. Every finding below is something that blocks her — and every fix is measured against whether it gets her from start to a paid $25.

03

How I know this isn't guesswork

Every finding below traces to something I saw, not a best-practice checklist. I drove the real flow and instrumented it.

  • 🧭End-to-end, twice. Registered a real applicant, verified by email, and completed all four steps to the checkout — once on desktop, once on an emulated iPhone.
  • 🔌Watched the wire. Network capture is how I found the real cause of the sign-up error (a rejected phone country code) and that conversion pixels fire on failed submits.
  • 📨Read the emails. Verified the sponsor-invoice flow all the way to the invoice that lands in the inbox and the payment portal it links to.
  • 🧪Probed edge cases. Deliberately uploaded a PDF, an 11MB file, a one-sided ID, and an invalid coupon to see how the flow fails — that's where friction hides.
  • 📊Data would quantify this. In a real role I'd start from Clarity session replays and Mixpanel funnels to see where drop concentrates. Without access, this walkthrough generates grounded hypotheses — quantify them with your funnel data. That's exactly why fixing the broken tracking (C2) is the first move: you can't optimise a number you're measuring wrong.

What's already working for conversion

Credit where due — cutting these would hurt. Keep them.

Frictionless sign-up intent

  • Single screen · honest "less than 10 minutes" expectation
  • One-tap Google path — also skips email verification
  • Passwordless throughout — nothing to invent or forget

The system knows its audience

  • Docs localize by country (NIMC ID, Voter's ID; "NiN slip" flagged)
  • Phone verification via WhatsApp
  • Sponsor invoicing · bank transfer · country-adjusted pricing

Guidance where it counts

  • Program descriptions + "See example" images guide choices
  • Clear 4-step progress rail sets expectations
  • Steps are clickable — go back and edit

It's recoverable — and it works

  • Log out / back in via OTP → dropped straight back at the last step
  • Every prior step intact, nothing re-entered
  • Essential for the heavy identity step — leave to find a doc, return safely

They chase the drop-offs

  • Next-day missed call + WhatsApp from a named "Application Specialist"
  • Quick replies + a scheduling offer
  • A human recovery loop — and why phone is collected early

Low-commitment program choice

  • Switch program at any step — even at checkout
  • No loss of progress
  • Picking wrong or comparison-shopping doesn't force a restart
05

The recovery loop — winning back the drop-offs

Not everyone who leaves is lost. Nexford runs a human-assisted, multi-channel re-engagement loop that pulls abandoned applicants back to finish and pay. It moves the same App start → App complete metric — from outside the UI — so it belongs in the plan.

Email — always on

  • 3–4 test accounts → each got a personalized follow-up
  • From a different named "Application Specialist" each time
  • Per-applicant ownership, not a generic blast

Call + WhatsApp

  • Next-day call
  • WhatsApp when unanswered — quick replies + scheduling
  • WhatsApp is where this audience actually replies

Why it works

  • Personal, human, trust-building (Amaka is deciding if a US school is real)
  • Recovered applications complete and pay the $25
  • A genuine conversion lever

In the specialist's words (one real follow-up email): "I'm curious to see if you are still interested in the MBA. Let me know either way and I can amend your application over here. What makes sense as a next step?… Still having doubts? Let's chat! Reply to this email or add me on WhatsApp." — with links to learner testimonials and the alumni-outcomes survey. Signed by a named Nexford Application Specialist.

Keep  The human, multi-channel loop

  • Don't dilute the personal tone or the named owner
  • A real strength for this audience

Confirmed  A 1-tap "Resume & pay" deep link

  • Today's CTA is "reply" / "book a time" — higher friction
  • Add a 1-tap link straight back to the last step
  • Plus "Pay now" for identity-cleared applicants — in email + WhatsApp

Confirmed  Instrument the loop

  • Currently unmeasured
  • Add recovery events (sent / opened / clicked / resumed / recovered)
  • See channel mix, timing, cost per recovered app

Bet  Automate the sequence

  • A named human per applicant caps volume
  • Automate a timed email + WhatsApp sequence, human escalation for high-intent
  • A/B automated vs human; measure recovery-attributed completions

Where starts leak — and why

Grouped by where they hit the funnel. Every one is tied back to the start→complete objective.

Read this as depth, not a to-do list. Three things move the number most — the sign-up dead-end (C1), the file friction at identity (F-files), and KYC sitting before payment (F1). The rest is catalogued to show I looked everywhere; it's sized in §12 and sequenced in §13. In Amaka’s words: “I filled everything and it just says an error occurred” (C1) · “my ID is a PDF and my photo’s too big — before I’ve even paid?” (F-files/F1) · “ready to pay but my card won’t do USD” (payment).

C1A completed sign-up can dead-end on a generic errorCriticalConfirmed
  • Saw — filled the whole form; “Register Now” returns only “An error occurred.” The phone selector offers 245 countries but the backend rejects some codes (Ukraine +380). Same class in a separate run: the code doesn’t follow the chosen city (Amsterdam + default +1 → “Invalid phone number”, no hint the selector is the problem).
  • Costs — a hard loss at the widest, highest-intent point; it contradicts the “100+ countries” promise. Evidence: 400 on /validate — “PhoneCountryCode 380 … condition not met”
don’t offer unsupported countries (or flag them); return a field-level, actionable message. The country limit may be intentional — the silent generic error is not.
C2Conversion pixels fire on failed sign-upsMeasurement integrityConfirmed
  • Saw — on a failed (400) submit, Google Ads / TikTok / Mixpanel / Ahrefs still fire.
  • Costs — the very metric this project optimizes is inflated by non-events, so ad spend and funnel dashboards read partly-fictional “starts.”
gate all lead/conversion events on a 2xx success. Not a direct lift — it’s what makes App start → App complete measurable; fix once, protect every downstream decision.
F-filesIdentity uploads reject PDFs, large photos, and HEIC — with a weak errorHighConfirmed
  • Saw — ID/selfie accept only png/jpg/jpeg ≤10MB. A PDF scan is rejected via a small corner toast; an 11MB photo fails for size; iPhone HEIC would fail too. The transcript step, by contrast, accepts PDF.
  • Costs — most affected users can’t convert files easily — at the step nearest payment. The image-only rule is correct (it feeds face-match/OCR); the friction is the fixable part.
normalize on the client — PDF→image, auto-compress >10MB, decode HEIC — so the KYC pipeline still gets a clean image and the user does nothing. See Engineering Brief #2.
F9A one-sided ID is accepted — front/back isn’t enforcedMedConfirmed: no enforcementInferred: post-pay rejection
  • Saw (confirmed) — the copy asks for “front and back as separate images,” but a single image was accepted through to checkout — no enforcement. Verification is asynchronous (dummy images passed 201, no real-time check).
  • Inferred (I didn’t pay, so a risk not a fact) — an incomplete ID would likely surface later, possibly after payment, as a re-upload — turning a won conversion into refund/support/churn.
two labeled slots (Front / Back), or document-type-aware (passport = one), enforced before payment. To confirm the cost, check when incomplete-ID rejections fire relative to payment.
E1“Not a native English speaker” dead-ends the form — the required field never rendersCriticalConfirmedSeparate run · re-verify
  • Saw (separate test run, 12 Jul — re-verify before shipping) — answering “No” to “Are you a native English speaker?” should reveal an English-Proficiency field. It never renders; submit returns “Your English Proficiency level is required” with no such field anywhere on the page. Toggling back to “Yes” clears the error — the rule exists, the field was never shipped.
  • Costs — an unrecoverable mid-funnel dead-end for exactly the population a 100+-country school serves. If the “No” segment is 25–40% of applicants, this could be the single largest leak in the funnel.
Fix render the conditional field (or drop the rule) — and adopt the invariant that validation may only require fields that are actually rendered, with a regression test. Size it via % of “No” answers vs. their step-completion.
V-UXRequired fields aren’t marked; errors can land off-screenMediumConfirmed
  • Saw — no asterisks — you learn a field is required by failing. On the long Personal-info step the inline error can sit below the fold, with no summary and no auto-scroll. The coupon field, notably, does show a clean inline error.
  • Costs — people read a silent no-op on “Save & Continue” as “the button’s broken” and bail mid-form.
  • Same family (12 Jul run) — choosing a program injects new fields that silently push “Register Now” down the page (misclick risk), and the Google path can return no last name with no flag on the empty required field.
mark required fields; on submit, scroll to and focus the first error. Reuse the coupon-field pattern that already works.
F7Great upload examples, hidden — and not localized as imagesMediumConfirmed
  • Saw — the “See example” modal is genuinely helpful, but it’s a small text link most people miss. And the example images stay US-style (US license, US passport) even for a Nigerian applicant, though the document list localizes.
  • Costs — wrong/blurry uploads get rejected → stalls at Step 3.
surface a thumbnail beside the dropzone; localize the example images to the detected country.
F14Bank-transfer details disagree between checkout and the invoiceMed–HighConfirmed
  • Saw — checkout shows “Bank of America, 222 Broadway”; the emailed PDF invoice for the same $25 wire says “Bank of New York, 22 Broadway.” Account, routing and SWIFT match — and the SWIFT (BOFAUS3N) is Bank of America — so the invoice bank name/address is wrong. (The email also says “tuition fees”; the invoice correctly says “Application Fee.”)
  • Costs — a sponsor doing an international wire has two conflicting beneficiaries at the exact payment moment.
one source of truth for wire details across checkout, invoice and email; confirm the correct bank from the SWIFT. Fix the “tuition” → “application fee” copy.
PerfThe sign-up screen is heavy before the audience can even startMed–HighConfirmed (measured)
  • Saw — register page on throttled Slow-3G: ~21s to load, ~10.6s to DOMContentLoaded, 516KB, 16 of 21 requests third-party. The Stripe SDK loads on register — four steps before anyone pays — with a Warmly widget, Mixpanel and web fonts.
  • Costs — for a mobile-first, emerging-market base on slow networks, that’s drop before App start even happens.
defer non-critical third-party — load Stripe only at checkout, lazy-load the widget and fonts. Validate against real-user (field) LCP.
F17Google sign-in doesn’t feel like signing in — and puts a token in the URLMedConfirmed
  • Saw (good) — “Continue with Google” is real OAuth with minimal scope, and it skips the email-OTP step — completing it drops you straight into the application.
  • Saw (friction) — right after Google auth you land on a register-styled screen (“Start your education journey”) headed “We need some additional information…” with empty fields — it reads as if sign-in failed.
  • Security (secondary, a privacy issue not a conversion lever) — the OAuth access token rides in the URL (?token=ya29…) — tokens in URLs leak via history, logs and referrers.
after Google, show a “Welcome, Fatih — 2 quick details” state, not a register screen; stop passing the token in the URL; normalize the ALL-CAPS name.

08

Bigger bets — validate before building

These could be the largest wins, but each may be an intentional trade-off. I'd bring a hypothesis and a measurement, not a mandate. Each below carries a cheap first test and a success threshold — and the prerequisite for all of them is instrumenting the funnel (fix tracking, add step-level analytics) so the bets can actually be judged.

Validate  Move KYC to after payment

  • Identity + selfie sit before the $25
  • Deferring past payment could lift completion most — but may be deliberate gating
  • Measure: Step-3 drop vs. fraud/quality cost of verifying later

Validate  Local payment rails

  • All methods are USD (card, PayPal, or a costly US wire)
  • Local currency / Paystack / mobile money could recover final-step drop
  • Measure: payment-failure rate by country

Validate  Trim the Personal-info step

  • 14+ fields, some duplicated from sign-up
  • Measure: which are truly required vs. accreditation vs. marketing
  • Defer or drop the rest

Validate  The "Awaiting Payment" framing

  • A green ✓ "Thank you for applying!" shows though payment is pending
  • Could read as done — or be an intentional flexible-payment play
  • Measure: payment rate after this screen

Validate  Align the acquisition promise

  • The form promises “less than 10 minutes” — then mid-flow asks for a government ID, a selfie and $25
  • An unmet promise makes the form absorb blame for a mismatch it didn’t create — name the real steps upfront (“10 min + ID + $25”) or remove the friction the promise assumes away
  • Measure: drop at the identity reveal, by acquisition channel

Who signs off the KYC-after-payment bet — it trades fraud exposure for conversion, so it isn't mine to mandate. I'd run it as a bounded A/B with a kill switch, sponsored by a small council: Compliance/Risk (KYC timing is their policy) + Finance/Payments (refund/chargeback economics) + Exec sponsor as arbiter, with the KYC vendor confirming feasibility. Ship only if App-complete rises with fraud/refunds flat or within an agreed tolerance.

Engineering briefs

Two changes, specified enough to build without a kickoff meeting. Acceptance criteria in Given/When/Then.

Both briefs are also packaged as standalone engineer hand-off docs with 1-click links into the prototype’s broken vs fixed states: ENG-1 · register dead-end →  ·  ENG-2 · file normalization →

Brief #1 — End the "unsupported country" dead-end

As an applicant from any country Nexford serves, I want to know upfront if my phone country is supported and get a clear message if not — so I can finish instead of hitting an unexplained error.

Scenario Unsupported country is prevented at the source
Given my country isn't on the supported list
When I open the phone-country selector
Then it's shown disabled with a short reason — I can't reach a state that will 400

Scenario Any validation error is field-level
Given the backend rejects a field (e.g. PhoneCountryCode)
When the response returns
Then the error shows inline on that field, the page focuses it, and the generic toast is gone

Scenario Pixels never fire on failure
Given a non-2xx submit When it's handled Then no lead/conversion pixels fire
Edge casesempty (client-validate first), loading (disable + spinner, no double-submit), 5xx (retryable banner), mobile (error scrolls into view), Google path enforces same rule
Depends oncanonical supported-country list (Admissions/Compliance); validate endpoint already returns a field key; move pixel calls into the success callback
Successregister-completion for affected countries rises toward the accepted-country baseline; zero generic error toasts on register; pixels verified to fire only on 2xx
Rolloutbehind a feature flag — field-level error first, then the disabled-country list; watch register drop by country for 2 weeks
Non-goalschanging which countries are supported — that's a policy decision (Admissions/Compliance), not this ticket
Open QsIs the limit sanctions/ops or a bug? Hide vs. disabled-with-reason? Is phone required at register at all?

Brief #2 — Client-side file normalization + Front/Back slots

As an applicant on my phone with my ID as a PDF or a large/HEIC photo, I want to upload whatever I have and have it made compliant automatically — so I'm not blocked or silently rejected right before paying.

Scenario PDF auto-converted
Given I select a PDF When it's added Then page 1 (and 2 → front/back) becomes an image with a preview; no error

Scenario Oversized / HEIC handled
Given a >10MB or .heic photo When added Then it's down-scaled/re-encoded to a compliant JPEG and accepted

Scenario Front & back enforced
Given a two-sided ID type When I continue with one image Then I'm blocked with an inline message (passport = one slot)
Edge casesmulti-page PDF, corrupt/locked PDF (inline error), keep text legible for OCR, camera-capture path uses same normalizer, compress before upload to save mobile data
Depends onpdf.js + canvas + HEIC decode (lazy-load on this step only); KYC pipeline expects images (confirmed); server already combines front+back
Successfirst-try upload acceptance ↑ and Step-3 format/size rejections ↓ materially; no OCR/face-match quality regression on converted images
Rolloutflag per file-type (PDF → HEIC → oversize) so any KYC-pipeline regression is isolable; check verification pass-rate in week 1
Non-goalsaltering ID content in any way — normalization only (format/size); KYC/anti-fraud integrity untouched
Open Qsauto-detect doc type vs. ask? client vs. server conversion for big PDFs? apply same normalizer to transcripts?

Measurement plan — what to instrument

Written for the engineer. You can't optimize — or scale — the funnel on a number you're measuring wrong: today conversion pixels fire on failed sign-ups (C2), so "App starts" are partly fictional. This is the prerequisite for every fix and every number in the impact model. Full schema and property types in TRACKING-PLAN.md.

Rule #1 — gate pixels on success (fixes C2). Move every lead/conversion call (Google Ads, TikTok, Mixpanel funnel steps) into the 2xx callback of app_started and app_completed. A failed submit fires nothing. Dedupe by application_id; stitch anonymous → identified at account creation.
