# Nexford University — Company Research (funnel context)
> Purpose: ground the audience/model context and the friction hypotheses for optimizing the ApplyNXU funnel (App start → App complete).

## Summary (what matters for the funnel)
- **What:** Washington DC–based, 100% online, **US-accredited**, competency-based next-gen university. Programs: BBA, MBA (+ Finance/Entrepreneurship), MSc (Data Analytics, AI & Tech Mgmt, Digital Transformation), AAS. **No pure CS/engineering** (confirmed in the walkthrough).
- **Audience:** **100+ countries**; strong **Africa/Nigeria** focus (3,000+ students in Nigeria, 400+ graduates; physical community space in Lagos). Target: **working professionals** in emerging markets who want a US degree without relocating.
- **Device:** **mobile-first** ("mobile-optimized learning", Canvas mobile app) → the application funnel must be flawless on mobile too.
- **Business model:** pay-as-you-go / **monthly subscription** ($200/mo for MBA), no loans, start on the 1st of any month. **Country-adjusted pricing** (no residency premium).
- **Price:** MBA total **$3,300–$8,100** (finish faster, pay less). **Application fee = $25** (all programs) → **"App complete" = this $25 checkout.**

## How it strengthens the friction hypotheses
1. **C1 (Ukraine +380 rejection) is more serious.** A school serving 100+ countries with country-adjusted pricing that offers a country in the UI but rejects it server-side → directly lost applicants; contradicts the global positioning.
2. **Phone default +1 (US) is wrong.** Audience is Africa/emerging-market heavy → every user must change the country.
3. **Mobile is critical.** Mobile-first audience → every step's mobile experience (uploads, dropdowns, error placement) directly affects conversion.
4. **English-only ID (F2) is nuanced.** Nigeria is English-speaking (less of an issue there), but Francophone Africa / LatAm / other of the 100+ countries → real barrier → passport required.
5. **Document/photo friction (F3–F8) is expensive.** Despite the low $25 barrier, placing heavy KYC (ID + selfie + plain background) BEFORE payment, for a low-digital-literacy + mobile audience, drops people at the highest-intent moment → strengthens the sequencing question (F1).
6. **"Finish faster and pay less" box is actually model-consistent.** In a monthly-subscription model, finishing faster = fewer months = less spend; prior-learning credit accelerates that. So it's not just a distraction — it's an upsell aligned with the business model (nuance: placement is debatable, logic is sound).
